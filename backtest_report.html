A. Cost sensitivity — mean net return per trade (bps). Taker fees alone ≈ 9bps round-trip.

The whole edge lives inside the fee. At a realistic 10–15bps, only longer holds (2–4h) and MID/LOW tiers stay clearly positive — but LOW tier's gross edge is a slippage mirage in thin books. HIGH tier at 1h is marginal.

D. Walk-forward decay — HIGH+MID, 1h hold, biweekly. The signal is fading within the sample.

Net-of-cost edge goes from +14.9bps → +7.4 → −4.1 → −6.4 across the four fortnights. Either regime-driven or crowding. This is the single biggest red flag: the strategy is not stationary over 52 days.

B. Exit rules — HIGH+MID, max 4h hold, 12bps cost

An asymmetric stop 1% / target 2% roughly quadruples per-trade Sharpe (+0.12) by letting the reversion run while capping the tail where a breakout is genuine — at the cost of a sub-50% hit rate.

E. Historical decay — the verdict — 1h signal, 175 perps, Dec 2025 → Jul 2026. Net edge per trade (bps), after 11bps cost.

The fade did not exist before June 2026. Net-of-cost edge was zero-to-negative every month from Dec through May (six straight losing months), then turned sharply positive in June and faded again through July. Over the full 8 months the HIGH+MID fade nets −10.9bps/trade (win 52.4%). The "edge" the 15m backtest found is a 2-month regime (Jun–Jul 2026), not a structural effect — and it was already decaying inside that window.

F. Maker execution & adverse selection — HIGH+MID, 2h hold. Resting limit to fade the breakout.

Adverse selection is real and large: the trades you miss (limit never fills) are the explosive reverters worth +90 to +145bps, while filled trades' raw edge shrinks to near zero. But the price improvement from posting into the breakout more than compensates on the filled subset — maker net (+9 to +25bps) matches or beats taker (+12bps), at the cost of filling only 58–94% of signals. Execution is not the blocker; the regime-dependence (panel E) is.

G. Regime filter — the payoff — 1h signal, 8 months. Which conditioning variable recovers a persistent edge?

Volatility is the primary filter (vol-only already +3.3bps, t=2.66). Funding magnitude does nothing and funding alone is negative — but funding direction (fade breakouts that run with crowded positioning) roughly doubles the vol-only edge. Momentum/EMA and efficiency-ratio filters were dead ends (not shown: flat or wrong-signed).

Stacked-filter net edge by month (bps). Positive in 5 of 8 months and — critically — positive in both the Dec–May "dead" era (+9.1) and Jun–Jul (+7.7), unlike the raw signal. But note Dec (n=43) and Mar are outliers doing real work, and Feb/May/Jul are flat-to-negative — this is a genuine but noisy edge, not a metronome. Survives cost to ~20bps breakeven. Extending the hold from 2h → 8h (panel H) lifts this to +24.9bps and 7/8 positive months.

H. Risk management — why stops kill it — stacked filter, 8h max hold, stop-width sweep

The tail is the premium. A hard stop cleanly caps the worst trade (−38% → −2%) and flips skew positive — but turns net negative at every width, because it realizes losses on trades that would have reverted. Even a 10% stop (which only triggers on genuine runaway breakouts) costs 40bps/trade. Conclusion: no per-trade price stop; hold to a time-based exit and manage the tail with sizing + diversification across many concurrent signals.
